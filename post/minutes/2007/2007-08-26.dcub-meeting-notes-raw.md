Agenda Item 8. Application Profile of Simple Dublin Core<br>
Question being addressed is "What do we want to say simple DC is?". This work
needs to be based on what functions we are trying to provide for.<br>
1. To provide an AP that tells implementors how to use the traditional '15' in a
way that conforms to modern understanding of metadata descriptions based on the
DCAM.<br>
2. Scope and purpose based on the mature understanding of the UB, developed over
the last 6 years.<br>
To be explicit about a number of things, to comment, to point out flaws and do
some house cleaning.<br>
Something that looks very much like the DC15; go into spatial and temporal;
provide a pedagological clarification of the 'core'.<br>
The new AP is not intended to solve the general problem of 'resource
description', which is best addressed by communities in Application Profiles.<br>
How to deal with source and identifier without going beyond the 15?<br>
NB We are not starting from scratch.<br>
Purpose (MN): to serve as a path for implementors of the simple DC to move
towards a more modern implementation of DC metadata terms.<br>
Need to have a relatively conservative interpretation of the OAI-PMH version of
DC.<br>
Need for a second profile - but what is its function and scope?<br>
Describe the legacy and the path to the new - in two profiles but presented as
one package.<br>
Straightforward to create a AP for traditional 'simple DC'. Then...?<br>
A second AP that provides a modern way of implementing what people think of as
'simple DC' (but called something else).<br>
To reverse engineer traditional use of 'simple' DC.<br>
Reconstruct a new version of what was called 'simple DC' that implements modern
understandings and ameliorates existing flaws in the DC15.<br>
APs or description set profiles?<br>
<br>

models everything as literals. Document the legacy functional requirements and
the organizational context for this AP.<br>
